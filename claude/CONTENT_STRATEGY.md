## Brand Voice

Consistent across every section, every persona, every device — the voice does not change, only the emphasis.

| Trait | Means | Does not mean |
|---|---|---|
| Confident | States capability plainly | Boastful, superlative-stacking |
| Precise | Specific claims, real nouns | Vague industry jargon |
| Warm | Human, partner-toned | Casual, jokey |
| Global | Comfortable discussing scale | Impersonal or corporate-generic |

Voice traces directly to the brand personality in [CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md#brand-personality): premium, modern, corporate, elegant, reliable, global, minimal, trustworthy.

## Tone

Tone shifts slightly by context, voice never does:

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hero / brand statements | Assured, declarative |
| Services / process copy | Clear, informative, structured |
| Trust/testimonial framing | Understated — let the proof speak |
| Form / conversion copy | Direct, reassuring, low-pressure |
| Error/validation messages | Plain, helpful, never blaming the user |

## Hero Copy Rules

1. Headline states the core value proposition in ≤ 10 words — no throat-clearing ("Welcome to...").
2. Subheadline adds specificity: what services, what scale, what differentiator — one sentence.
3. Never open with a question, a pun, or an industry cliché ("In today's fast-paced world...").
4. The headline must survive being read with zero visual context — it should make sense as plain text.

**Example — good:**
> "Global freight, handled like it's the only shipment that matters."

**Example — rejected (generic template energy):**
> "Your Trusted Partner in Logistics Solutions"

## CTA Rules

Copywriting rules for CTA text — placement and visual rules live in [UX_GUIDELINES.md](UX_GUIDELINES.md#cta-rules).

- CTA copy is a verb phrase describing the specific next action: "Request a Quote," not "Submit" or "Learn More" as a default.
- Never use unexplained urgency ("Act Now!") — this is a B2B trust play, not a discount funnel.
- Secondary CTA copy names the alternative path clearly: "Talk to a Specialist," not "Contact Us."
- Microcopy under form submit buttons sets expectations: "We typically respond within one business day."

---

## Headline Rules

- Section headlines are short (3–7 words), declarative, and specific to that section's job (see [UX_GUIDELINES.md](UX_GUIDELINES.md#section-hierarchy)).
- Never reuse the same sentence structure for more than two consecutive section headlines — avoid the "Why Choose Us / Why Choose Our Team / Why Choose Our Process" pattern.
- Headlines describe outcomes or capability, not internal process names ("Reliable Delivery, Every Lane" beats "Our Logistics Methodology").

---

## Writing Style

- Active voice, present tense, by default.
- Short sentences preferred; a longer sentence is allowed only when it reads better aloud than two short ones would.
- No filler adjectives ("world-class," "cutting-edge," "innovative") without a concrete fact attached.
- Numbers and specifics beat adjectives: "12 trade lanes across Asia and Europe" beats "extensive global network" (pending confirmed data — see [BUSINESS_CONTEXT.md](BUSINESS_CONTEXT.md)).
- Oxford comma, sentence case for headings (not Title Case), consistent throughout.

---

## Content Hierarchy

Within any section, copy follows this order of authority:

1. **Headline** — the single claim
2. **Subheadline/support line** — the proof or specificity behind the claim
3. **Body copy** — expands only if necessary; many sections need none
4. **CTA/microcopy** — the next action

If a section needs more than ~40 words of body copy to make its point, the point is likely wrong for a marketing site — move detail to a future insights/content section (see [PROJECT.md](PROJECT.md#future-scope)) rather than padding the page.

## Messaging Framework

| Layer | Message |
|---|---|
| Category | Global B2B logistics partner |
| Differentiation | Reliability + transparency + personal account ownership, not lowest price |
| Reason to believe | Process transparency, confirmed trade-lane depth, direct account management |
| Emotional payoff | The buyer looks competent to their own stakeholders for choosing SK Internationals |

Every section's copy should be traceable to one row of this table. If it isn't, question whether the section belongs — see [Value Proposition](BUSINESS_CONTEXT.md#value-proposition).

### Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why it's rejected |
|---|---|
| Stacked superlatives ("best-in-class, world-leading, premier") | Reads as unverified marketing noise |
| Copy identical in structure across every service card | Signals templated content, undermines premium positioning |
| Passive, hedged claims ("We aim to try to deliver...") | Undermines the reliability positioning |
| Jargon without translation ("end-to-end omnichannel logistics synergy") | Alienates a buyer trying to solve a concrete problem |
